SubjectGuest lecture proposal — cognitive science of fake news & disinformation
Hello, My name is Antonin Atger. I am a French author and PhD student in cognitive science, specialising in disinformation and the psychological mechanisms behind the spread of fake news. I will be in the Guangzhou area in June — I am giving talks at the Lycée Français International Victor-Segalen in Hong Kong while there — and would love to explore the possibility of a guest lecture at Sun Yat-sen University. I have been giving talks on fake news and critical thinking for several years — in schools, public libraries, and conferences across France (86 schools, 36 libraries). My approach connects cognitive psychology (biases, heuristics, optical illusions) with a practical media literacy angle: why we fall for misinformation, and what we can actually do about it. I also cover disinformation beyond fake news — astroturfing, manufactured doubt, framing — and the limits of fact-checking as a standalone tool. I gave a TEDx talk on social media and critical thinking, and participated in an academic congress on disinformation dynamics alongside French researchers and science communicators.
